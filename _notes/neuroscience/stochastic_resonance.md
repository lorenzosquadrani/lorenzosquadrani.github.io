Neurons can exhibit a peculiar phenomenon, in which the addition of noise to the input signal can actually improve the signal-to-noise ratio of the output signal.

The input signal is a sequence of spikes, and the output signal is the membrane potential of the neuron.
The response of the neuron is highly nonlinear: the effect of multiple spikes can be much larger than the sum of the effects of individual spikes as the membrane potential reaches the threshold for firing an action potential.
