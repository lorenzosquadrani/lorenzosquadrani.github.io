Here I review the theoretical framework developed by Stefano Fusi and collaborators to interpret neural activity.

In particular I focus on the following papers:

- Bernardi, Silvia, et al. "The geometry of abstraction in the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex." Cell 183.4 (2020): 954-967.
- Courellis, Hristos S., et al. "Abstract representations emerge in human hippocampal neurons during inference." Nature (2024): 1-9.
- Fascianelli, Valeria, et al. "Neural representational geometries reflect behavioral differences in monkeys and recurrent neural networks." Nature Communications 15.1 (2024): 6479.
- Rigotti, Mattia, et al. "The importance of mixed selectivity in complex cognitive tasks." Nature 497.7451 (2013): 585-590.
- Fusi, Stefano, et al. "Spike-driven synaptic plasticity: theory, simulation, VLSI implementation." Neural computation 12.10 (2000): 2227-2258.
